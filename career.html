Albert Einstein

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Career

Albert Einstein's career was marked by groundbreaking scientific contributions and an enduring impact on modern physics. After graduating, he initially struggled to find an academic position and worked at the Swiss Patent Office. During this period, he published some of his most influential papers.

Swiss Patent Office:
Einstein worked as a technical assistant, which gave him time to think deeply about theoretical physics. This period led to the publication of the "Annus Mirabilis" papers in 1905.

Academic Posts:
He held positions at the University of Zurich, Charles University in Prague, and the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich. In 1914, he was appointed to the Prussian Academy of Sciences in Berlin.

General Theory of Relativity:
In 1915, Einstein presented his general theory of relativity, fundamentally changing our understanding of gravity and space-time.

Later Years:
Fleeing Nazi Germany, Einstein moved to the U.S. in 1933, where he joined the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. He continued to work on unified field theory and was active in political and humanitarian causes until his death in 1955.
