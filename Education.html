Education

Albert Einstein's education was a mix of traditional schooling and self-directed learning. He initially attended Catholic elementary school and then Luitpold Gymnasium in Munich, where he found the rigid teaching style stifling. He later attended the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich, where he focused on mathematics and physics, eventually earning a teaching diploma. Despite his brilliance, he faced challenges in the traditional academic system, but ultimately found success through his own intellectual pursuits.

Early Schooling
Einstein began his education at a Catholic elementary school in Munich and later attended Luitpold Gymnasium.

Rejection of Authoritarian Teaching:
He clashed with the strict, rote-learning focused environment of the Gymnasium, believing it stifled creativity.

Independent Learning:
He spent a year studying independently and later passed the entrance exam for the Polytechnic, graduating in 1900 with a degree in mathematics and physics.

Academic Career:
He later became a Privatdozent, then Professor Extraordinary at Zurich, and eventually held professorships in Prague and Berlin.
